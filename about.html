Golden Hour

Freestyle photography, as a personal habit, is my way of documenting life without rigid structure or predefined rules. Instead of planning every frame, I rely on intuition, timing, and emotional awareness to guide my shots. I am drawn to spontaneous moments — shifting light between buildings, reflections on wet streets, candid human expressions, or subtle textures in urban spaces.

Over time, this practice has strengthened my patience and visual sensitivity. It encourages me to explore unfamiliar places, experiment with angles, and trust creative instincts over technical rigidity. Through freestyle photography, I refine both technical skill and artistic confidence while learning to tell visual stories naturally.
